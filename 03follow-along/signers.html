Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

The Declaration of Independence was signed by 56 delegates representing the 13 American colonies in 1776. While every signer played a role in shaping American history, some became especially famous because of their later contributions to the country.

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Main author of the Declaration and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Respected diplomat, inventor, and the oldest signer at age 70.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress, known for his bold and iconic signature.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Organizer of the Boston Tea Party and a key voice for colonial resistance.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution calling for independence in the Continental Congress.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer and the longest-living signer, dying in 1832 at age 95.

The signers of the Declaration of Independence showed great courage by standing up for freedom, knowing they risked their lives. Famous figures like Thomas Jefferson and John Adams helped shape the nation, but all 56 signers played an important role in founding the United States. Their bravery and belief in liberty continue to inspire us today.
